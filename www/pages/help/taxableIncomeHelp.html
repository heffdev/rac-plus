Taxable Income (TI) is the amount on which you must pay Federal taxes. Though this calculation is geared towards US investors, there are many similarities to other tax systems.

Taxable Income = NOI - MI - PD - CAD - A + EI

Where:

  • NOI is the Net Operating Income
  • MI is the Mortgage Interest paid
  • PD is the Depreciation, Real Property
  • CAD is the Depreciation, Capital Additions
  • A is the Amortization, Points and Closing Costs
  • EI is the Earned Interest

For example:

Variable Amount
NOI 30,000
less MI 20,400
less PD 7,218
less CAD 100
Less A 200
plus EI 185
= TI 2,267
