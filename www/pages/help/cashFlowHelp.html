Cash Flow (CF) is the movement of money into or out of a business, project, or financial product. It is usually measured during a specified, limited period of time. Measurement of cash flow can be used for calculating other parameters that give information on a company's value and situation.

CF = NOI - DS - CA + LP + EI - TL

Where:

  • NOI is the Net Operating Income
  • DS is the Debt Service paid
  • CA are the Capital Additions
  • LP is the Loan Proceeds
  • EI is the Earned Interest
  • TL is the Tax Liability

For example:

Variable Amount
NOI 30,000
less DS 20,500
less CA 0
plus LP 100
plus EI 85
= CF 3,545
