The Net Income Multiplier or NIM is a factor that is used to estimate the market value of income producing properties. NIM is used to express the relationship between a property's value and its net operating income (NOI) for the current or coming year

NIM = 1 /Cap Rate

Were:

  • Cap Rate is the Capitalization Rate

The NIM represents the amount that a typical investor would pay for each dollar of NOI. To use the NIM to obtain a properties present value (PV) you use:

PV = NIM x NOI

Where:

  • NIM is the Net Income Multiplier
  • NOI is the Net Operating Income
