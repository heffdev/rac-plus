Net Operating Income (NOI) is a property's income after being reduced by vacancy and credit loss and all operating expenses. If this is a positive value, it is referred to as net operating income, while a negative value is called a net operating loss (NOL).

NOI = (GSI x VCL) - OE

Example:

  • GSI is the total Gross Scheduled Income
  • VCL is the estimated percentage of Vacancy and Credit Loss
  • OE is the Operating Expenses

NOI is often viewed as a good measure of property performance and is one of the most important calculations relevant to real estate investment.
