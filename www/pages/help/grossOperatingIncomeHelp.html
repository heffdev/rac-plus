The Gross Operating Income (GOI) - also called Effective Gross Income (EGI) - is the amount of income produced by a piece of property, plus miscellaneous income, less vacancy costs and collection losses. Gross Operating Income is a metric commonly used to evaluate the value of a piece of investment property.

GOI = GSI x VCL

Example:

  • GSI is the total Gross Scheduled Income
  • VCL is the estimated percentage of Vacancy and Credit Loss

GSI = 500,000

VCL = 20%
